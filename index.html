Generative Models for
General-Purpose Robots

ICLR 2025 Workshop, April 27, 2025

Introduction

Generative models have emerged as powerful tools for creating diverse and novel data, enabling significant advancements in various domains, including vision, language, and robotics. In the context of robotics, generative models hold the potential to revolutionize how robots perceive, reason, and interact with the world, offering a pathway to truly general-purpose robots capable of performing a wide range of tasks. These models allow robots to autonomously generate behaviors, anticipate future states, and generalize across tasks and environmentsâ€”key features needed for robust real-world applications. However, many challenges remain in leveraging generative models for robotics, such as generating realistic and diverse behaviors in unstructured environments, integrating multi-modal sensory information, and ensuring real-time performance in dynamic scenarios.

This workshop aims to bring together researchers from both academia and industry to discuss the latest advancements and challenges in applying generative models to general-purpose robots. Key topics include generating task-agnostic behaviors, long-horizon planning, multi-task learning, and bridging the gap between simulation and real-world performance. Researchers will share their work through talks, posters, and discussions, exploring how generative models can address current limitations in robot adaptability and generalization.

We will explore the following focused research questions:

Generative Models for Imitation Learning and Reinforcement Learning
  • How can generative models be used to autonomously generate diverse and realistic robot behaviors?
  • What techniques can improve generalization to novel tasks and environments?
Multi-Modal Perception
  • How can robots effectively fuse data from multiple sensory modalities using generative models?
  • What are the best strategies for incorporating uncertainty in perception for general-purpose robots?
Planning and Control
  • How can generative models assist in long-horizon planning and decision-making?
  • What role do generative models play in dynamic, unstructured environments?
Real-World Deployment
  • How can we bridge the gap between simulated environments and real-world robot performance?
  • What are the main obstacles in deploying generative models on physical robots in real-time scenarios?

This workshop is designed to attract researchers from academia and industry interested in the intersection of generative models and robotics. Through presentations and discussions, participants will explore how generative models can transform general-purpose robots and push the boundaries of robot autonomy.
