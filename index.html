Generative Models for
General-Purpose Robots

RSS 2025 Workshop, June 25, 2025

Introduction

Recent advances in generative models have emerged as powerful tools for modeling diverse and novel data, enabling significant advancements in various domains, including text generation and image and video synthesis. In the context of robotics, generative models hold the potential to revolutionize how robots perceive, reason, and interact with the world, offering a pathway to truly general-purpose robots capable of performing a wide range of tasks. These models allow robots to learn and model the behaviors from multi-modal data, anticipate future world states, automatically synthesize diverse environments and demonstrations, and generalize across tasks and environmentsâ€”key features needed for robust real-world applications.

However, many challenges remain in leveraging generative models for building general-purpose robots, such as modeling realistic and diverse behaviors in unstructured demonstrations, integrating and learning multi-modal sensory information, long-horizon behavior synthesis, physical-aware contact modeling, and ensuring real-time performance in dynamic scenarios, and there also remains untapped potential in combining generative models with robot learning algorithms.

This workshop aims to bring together researchers from both generative models and robotics to discuss the latest advancements and challenges in applying generative models to general-purpose robots. Key topics include generating multi-modal behaviors, long-horizon planning, multi-task learning, and bridging the gap between simulation and real-world performance. Researchers will share their work through talks, posters, and discussions, exploring how generative models can address current limitations in robot adaptability and generalization.

We will explore the following focused research questions:

Generative Models for Imitation Learning and Reinforcement Learning
  • How can generative models be used to model complex and multi-modal behaviors from data and interactions?
  • How to improve generalization to novel tasks and environments?
Multi-Modal Perception
  • How can robots effectively fuse data from multiple sensory modalities using generative models?
  • How to deal with the embodiment gap and data conflicts from diverse robot dataset sources?
Dataset and Environments
  • How to automatically generate large-scale high-quality synthetic datasets for general-purpose robots?
  • How to fine-tune the trained policy with online interactions?
Planning and Control
  • How can generative models assist in long-horizon planning and decision-making?
  • How to build physical-aware generative models for control?
Real-World Deployment
  • How can we bridge the gap between simulated environments and real-world robot performance?
  • What are the main obstacles in deploying generative models on physical robots in real-time scenarios?

This workshop is designed to attract researchers from academia and industry interested in the intersection of generative models and robotics. Through presentations and discussions, participants will explore how generative models can transform general-purpose robots and push the boundaries of robot autonomy.

      Call for Papers

      The goal of this workshop is to bring together researchers from diverse fields, including machine learning, robotics, control, perception, and hardware design, who are working on applying generative models to enhance robot capabilities. We encourage submissions that present novel contributions or insights into how generative models can drive advancements in general-purpose robots. We welcome papers on the following (but not limited to) topics: (the list is not exhaustive):

      • Generative models for robotic behavior generation
      • Generative methods for synthetic dataset and environment generation
      • Multi-modal sensor fusion using generative models for robot decision-making
      • Applications of generative models in robot perception, reasoning, and control
      • Generative planning and long-horizon trajectory generation
      • Integration of generative models with real-time robotic systems
      • Bridging the sim-to-real gap with generative models for robot training and deployment
      • Reinforcement learning and generative models for adaptive behavior
      • Applications of generative models in robotic manipulation, navigation, and interaction
      • Data-efficient learning using generative models in robotics
